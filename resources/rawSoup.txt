         <p class="mw-empty-elt">
         </p>
         <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1096954695/mw-parser-output/.tmulti">

         <p>
          This list of current
          <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">
           National Football League
          </a>
          (NFL) stadiums includes their locations, capacities, their first year of usage, and home teams. Although the NFL has 32 teams, there are only 30 full-time NFL stadiums. This is because the
          <a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants" title="New York Giants">
           New York Giants
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/New_York_Jets" title="New York Jets">
           New York Jets
          </a>
          share
          <a href="/wiki/MetLife_Stadium" title="MetLife Stadium">
           MetLife Stadium
          </a>
          , and the
          <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">
           Los Angeles Rams
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Chargers" title="Los Angeles Chargers">
           Los Angeles Chargers
          </a>
          share
          <a href="/wiki/SoFi_Stadium" title="SoFi Stadium">
           SoFi Stadium
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="thumb tmulti tright">
          <div class="thumbinner multiimageinner" style="width:488px;max-width:488px">
           <div class="trow">
            <div class="theader" style="text-align:center">
             Newest Stadiums in the NFL
            </div>

              , opened in 2020. It is the home of the

         <p>
          The newest full-time NFL stadiums are
          <a href="/wiki/SoFi_Stadium" title="SoFi Stadium">
           SoFi Stadium
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">
           Inglewood, California
          </a>
          , home of both the
          <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">
           Los Angeles Rams
          </a>
          and the
          <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Chargers" title="Los Angeles Chargers">
           Los Angeles Chargers
          </a>
          ; and
          <a href="/wiki/Allegiant_Stadium" title="Allegiant Stadium">
           Allegiant Stadium
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Paradise,_Nevada" title="Paradise, Nevada">
           Paradise, Nevada
          </a>
          , home of the
          <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Raiders" title="Las Vegas Raiders">
           Las Vegas Raiders
          </a>
          . Both stadiums opened for the
          <a href="/wiki/2020_NFL_season" title="2020 NFL season">
           2020 season
          </a>
          .
          <a href="/wiki/Soldier_Field" title="Soldier Field">
           Soldier Field
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">
           Chicago
          </a>
          is the oldest, having opened in 1924; however, the Bears did not play at Soldier Field until 1971 and did not play there in 2002 while the stadium was under reconstruction, and thus the oldest continuously used stadium in the NFL is
          <a href="/wiki/Lambeau_Field" title="Lambeau Field">
           Lambeau Field
          </a>
          , hosting the
          <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers" title="Green Bay Packers">
           Green Bay Packers
          </a>
          since its opening in 1957.
         </p>
         <p>
          The NFL uses several other stadiums on a regular basis in addition to the teams' designated regular home sites. In England, two
          <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
           London
          </a>
          venues –
          <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">
           Wembley Stadium
          </a>
          and from 2016 to 2018 the
          <a href="/wiki/Twickenham_Stadium" title="Twickenham Stadium">
           Twickenham Stadium
          </a>
          then the
          <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_Stadium" title="Tottenham Hotspur Stadium">
           Tottenham Hotspur Stadium
          </a>
          – are contracted to host a combined four games per season, as part of the
          <a href="/wiki/NFL_International_Series" title="NFL International Series">
           NFL International Series
          </a>
          which runs through 2022.
          <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Azteca" title="Estadio Azteca">
           Estadio Azteca
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">
           Mexico City
          </a>
          hosted NFL International Series games in 2016, 2017, and 2019, and was under contract to host one game per season through 2021. In addition,
          <a href="/wiki/Tom_Benson_Hall_of_Fame_Stadium" title="Tom Benson Hall of Fame Stadium">
           Tom Benson Hall of Fame Stadium
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Canton,_Ohio" title="Canton, Ohio">
           Canton, Ohio
          </a>
          , is the location of the annual exhibition
          <a href="/wiki/Pro_Football_Hall_of_Fame_Game" title="Pro Football Hall of Fame Game">
           Pro Football Hall of Fame Game
          </a>
          . From 2022 also the
          <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">
           Allianz Arena
          </a>
          in
          <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
           Germany
          </a>
          will host the Munich Game of the NFL International series.
         </p>
         <p>
          The majority of current NFL stadiums have sold
          <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">
           naming rights
          </a>
          to corporations. Only 5 of the league's 30 stadiums—Arrowhead Stadium, Cleveland Browns Stadium, Commanders Field, Lambeau Field, and Soldier Field—do not currently use a corporate-sponsored name. Though the Chiefs sold naming rights of the football field to
          <a href="/wiki/GEHA" title="GEHA">
           GEHA
          </a>
          , the team retain stadium branding under the Arrowhead name.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
           <a href="#cite_note-1">
            [1]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </p>

        <h2 class="section-heading" onclick="mfTempOpenSection(1)">
         <span class="indicator mf-icon mf-icon-expand mf-icon--small">
         </span>
         <span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium_characteristics">
          Stadium characteristics
         </span>
         <span class="mw-editsection">
          <a class="cdx-button cdx-button--size-large cdx-button--fake-button cdx-button--fake-button--enabled cdx-button--icon-only cdx-button--weight-quiet" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_current_NFL_stadiums&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" role="button" title="Edit section: Stadium characteristics">
           <span class="minerva-icon minerva-icon--edit">
           </span>
           <span>
            edit
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </h2>
        <section class="mf-section-1 collapsible-block" id="mf-section-1">
         <p>
          <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">
           Stadiums
          </a>
          represent a considerable expense to a community, and thus their construction, use, and funding often enter the public discourse.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_2-0">
           <a href="#cite_note-Economist-2">
            [2]
           </a>
          </sup>
          Also, given the perceived
          <a href="/wiki/Home_advantage" title="Home advantage">
           advantage
          </a>
          a team gets to playing in its home stadium, particular attention is given in the media to the peculiarities of each stadium's environment. Climate, playing surface (either natural or
          <a href="/wiki/Artificial_turf" title="Artificial turf">
           artificial turf
          </a>
          ), and the type of roof all contribute to giving each team its home-field advantage.
         </p>
         <p>
          Stadiums are either open, enclosed, or have a retractable roof. For retractable roofs, the home team determines if the roof is to be opened or closed 90 minutes before kickoff. The roof remains open unless
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">
           precipitation
          </a>
          or
          <a href="/wiki/Lightning" title="Lightning">
           lightning
          </a>
          is within the vicinity of the stadium, the
          <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">
           temperature
          </a>
          drops below 40 °F (4 °C), or
          <a href="/wiki/Wind_speed" title="Wind speed">
           wind gusts
          </a>
          are greater than 40 miles per hour (64 km/h), in which case the roof operators will close the roof.
          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
           <a href="#cite_note-3">
            [3]
           </a>
          </sup>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span class="mw-headline" id="Seating">
           Seating
          </span>
          <span class="mw-editsection">
           <a class="cdx-button cdx-button--size-large cdx-button--fake-button cdx-button--fake-button--enabled cdx-button--icon-only cdx-button--weight-quiet" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_current_NFL_stadiums&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" role="button" title="Edit section: Seating">
            <span class="minerva-icon minerva-icon--edit">
            </span>
            <span>
             edit
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </h3>
         <p>
          With a peak capacity of over 100,000 spectators (80,000+ listed seating capacity),
          <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Stadium" title="AT&amp;T Stadium">
           AT&amp;T Stadium
          </a>
          has the highest capacity of any NFL stadium, while
          <a href="/wiki/MetLife_Stadium" title="MetLife Stadium">
           MetLife Stadium
          </a>
          has the highest listed seating capacity at 82,500. The smallest stadium is
          <a href="/wiki/Soldier_Field" title="Soldier Field">
           Soldier Field
          </a>
          with a capacity of 61,500.
         </p>
         <p>
          In their normal configurations, all of the league's 30 stadiums have a seating capacity of at least 60,000 spectators; of those, a majority (17) have fewer than 70,000 seats, while 8 have between 70,000 and 80,000, and 5 can seat 80,000 or more. In contrast to
          <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">
           college football
          </a>
          stadiums, the largest of which can and regularly do accommodate over 100,000 spectators, no stadium in the league currently has a listed seating capacity of more than 82,500. Teams rarely build their stadiums far beyond the 80,000 seat threshold (and even then, only in the largest
          <a href="/wiki/Media_market" title="Media market">
           markets
          </a>
          ) because of the league's
          <a href="/wiki/Blackout_(broadcasting)" title="Blackout (broadcasting)">
           blackout
          </a>
          policy, which prohibited the
          <a href="/wiki/NFL_on_television" title="NFL on television">
           televising of any NFL game
          </a>
          within 75 miles of its home market if a game does not sell all of its non-premium seating. The policy has been suspended since 2015; from then until 2019, several teams played in temporary facilities with capacities far larger than a normal stadium. In 2020,
          <a href="/wiki/Social_distancing" title="Social distancing">
           social distancing
          </a>
          mandates related to the COVID-19 pandemic prohibited teams from selling out their stadiums, and several teams had no fans in attendance all season due to state mandates (for example, the Las Vegas Raiders had no spectators for the entire season in accordance with Nevada policy). The league has a firm minimum on the number of seats an NFL stadium should have; since 1971, the league has generally not allowed any stadium under 50,000 seats to host a full-time NFL team. In normal circumstances, all NFL stadiums are
          <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-seater" title="All-seater">
           all-seaters
          </a>
          .
         </p>
        </section>
